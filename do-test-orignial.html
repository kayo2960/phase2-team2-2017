Chess and Checkers House

If you're up for a game of chess, checkers, dominoes or backgammon, Central Park's Chess and Checkers House has 24 tables available. Feel free to borrow pieces or bring your own. You might even catch some grandmaster chess players on certain weekends. FYI, each grandmaster can play up to 30 people at a time.

Where: Mid-Park @ 64th Stree
When: Days and hours vary depending on the season.
Cost: Free to use tables and borrow pieces!
Who: Open to the Public, first come first served.

GMA's Summer Concerts

If you don't mind being up and at the park before 6am, come be part of a live taping of Good Morning America's Summer Concert Series. Just remember to bring your own breakfast or snack for while you're waiting in the long line. And, you might even be on TV too!

Where: Rumsey Playfield near 5th Ave. & 72nd Street
Directions: Enter on 5th Ave. at 69th Street
When: Fridays, line starts at 6am or earlier
Cost: Free! No tickets required uless otherwise noted.
Who: Open to the Public, first come first served.
Note: Certain concerts are ticketed but still free.

Victorian Gardens Amusement Park

Yes! There most certainly is an actual amusement park inside Central Park. During the hot months in NYC, Wollman Rink gets transformed into an amusement park fully equipped with traditional rides and games.

Where: Wollman Rink
Directions: Enter on 5th Ave. at 69th Street
When: Hours vary daily but generally between 10am - 9pm
Cost:
Weekdays $8.50 + rides & games or $24.50 unlimited rides
Weekends $9.50 + rides & games or $27.50 unlimited rides
Under 36" free admission plus rides and games.
Who: little kids, big kids and some tweens
Note: Certain concerts are ticketed but still free.

Row Boating

Row, row, row your boat, gently down the stream. Well, actually on the lake. The Loeb Boathouse has approximately 100 row boats for your rowing pleasure. Each boat seats four people so you can take turns building your upper body strength.

Where: East 72nd Street & Park Drive North
Directions: Enter at E. 72nd Street and 5th Ave.
When: 10am - 6pm, April to November, weather permitting
Cost: $15 for the first hour, $4 for each additional 15 minutes plus $20 deposit, CASH ONLY
Who: Children under 12 must be accompanied by an adult.

Whisper Bench

Do you have a secret you want to tell? Grab a friend. Sit on one side of the bench and have your friend sit on the opposite side. Whisper something to your friend. Your friend will hear whatever you whispered. By the way, it's officially known as The Charles B. Stover Bench.

Where: Shakespeare Garden between 79th and 80th
Directions: Enter at Central Park West and 72nd Street
When: Park Hours
Cost: Free!
Who: Open to the Public

Catch and Release Fishing

Of course New Yorkers go fishing! The best place to catch and release fish is at Harlem Meer. You can borrow a fishing pole at Charles A. Dana Discovery Center and get free bait too. And if you don't know how to fish, you can get free instructions.

Where: Charles A. Dana Discovery Center
Directions: Enter at 5th Ave. and 110th Street
When: 10am - 3pm
Cost: Free including bait and pole to borrow!
Who: Open to the Public
